Evaluating 1 + 2 + 3 + ...

We first find out the value of 1 - 2 + 3 - 4 + 5 - 6 + .... To this end, we recall the following series: \[ \frac{1}{(1 + x)^2} = 1 - 2x + 3x^2 - 4x^3 + 5x^4 - \cdots . \] Put \(x = 1\) we obtain \[ \frac{1}{4} = 1 - 2 + 3 - 4 + 5 - \cdots . \] Now (1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + ...) - (1 - 2 + 3 - 4 + ...) = 2(2 + 4 + 6 + 8 + ...) = 4(1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + ...), so that -1/4 = 3(1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + ...) and we get 1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + ... = -1/12.
